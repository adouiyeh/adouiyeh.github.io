I recently completed Anthropic's *AI Fluency: Framework & Foundations* course, and it reframed how I think about working with AI tools like Claude. Instead of treating AI as a search engine or a magic answer box, the course teaches a repeatable framework for **collaborating** with AI effectively, efficiently, ethically, and safely.

Here are the core ideas I'm taking with me.

## The 4D Framework

The course centers on four interconnected competencies:

- **Delegation** — knowing *which* tasks are worth handing to AI in the first place, and which aren't.
- **Description** — communicating context, constraints, and intent clearly enough that the AI can actually help.
- **Discernment** — critically evaluating AI output instead of accepting the first draft at face value.
- **Diligence** — taking responsibility for how AI-assisted work is used, verified, and disclosed.

## Three Modes of Collaboration

One distinction that stuck with me was the difference between three modes of working with AI:

- **Automation** — letting AI complete a task with minimal oversight.
- **Augmentation** — using AI as a thought partner while staying in the driver's seat.
- **Agency** — delegating multi-step, semi-autonomous work while still setting the boundaries.
